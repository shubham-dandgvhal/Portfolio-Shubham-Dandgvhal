Real-Time Operations Dashboard

A single-pane-of-glass dashboard built at IQVIA that centralizes Incident, Request, and Change ticket status alongside live queue counts — giving management instant insight into team load and delivery health.

RoleLead developer & product advisor
OrganizationIQVIA R&DS India
TypeInternal operational tool

The problem

Before the dashboard existed, leadership had to piece together operational status from ServiceNow reports, spreadsheets, and ad-hoc pings in chat. By the time the picture was complete, it was already out of date — and high-severity tickets could sit in the wrong queue without anyone noticing.

The approach

  • Interviewed managers and team leads to agree on the five to six metrics that actually drove daily decisions.
  • Designed a layout that made queue counts, ageing tickets, and change status scannable at a glance.
  • Built the UI with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, pulling live data from ServiceNow via its REST APIs.
  • Added colour-coded SLA indicators and a "what changed since last refresh" strip so the view feels alive.

Impact

  • Accelerated management decision-making with instant operational visibility.
  • Eliminated manual status round-ups that previously consumed multiple hours each week across team leads.
  • Improved oversight of Incident, Request, and Change queues in a highly regulated CRO/healthcare environment.

What I learned

  • The dashboard that helps leadership is the one that answers their next question, not the one with the most widgets.
  • Trust in a live view is earned through little details — timestamps, refresh status, and clear empty states.
  • Partnering early with stakeholders beats shipping a technically clever tool nobody uses.
