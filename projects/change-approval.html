Change Approval Lead-Time Reduction

Re-engineered the change-approval workflow so lead time dropped from 3–4 days to 24 hours — unblocking data-issue resolution and directly supporting timely global product delivery across multiple markets.

RoleProcess owner & tech advisor
OrganizationIQVIA R&DS India
TypeProcess & tooling improvement

The problem

Changes that needed to land quickly — especially data fixes tied to in-flight study milestones — regularly sat in the approval queue for three to four days. In a regulated CRO/healthcare context, that delay wasn't just an inconvenience; it risked slipping global go-lives.

The approach

  • Mapped the existing flow end-to-end and identified the two approval hops that caused most of the wait time.
  • Worked with stakeholders to agree clear approval criteria so reviewers could act without re-requesting context.
  • Introduced a fast-track lane for low-risk, pre-approved change types with a documented rollback plan.
  • Tightened the CAB cadence and added a lightweight async approval path in ServiceNow for out-of-cycle changes.
  • Built reporting around lead-time so the team could see the improvement — and catch regressions early.

Impact

  • Change-approval lead time reduced from 3–4 days to 24 hours.
  • Accelerated resolution of data issues that previously blocked release readiness.
  • Supported on-time, successful global delivery of the product across multiple markets.

What I learned

  • Most process delays come from ambiguity, not strictness — clearer criteria move things faster than looser rules.
  • Pair every process change with a metric, or you'll have no way to prove it's working a month later.
  • In regulated environments, "fast" and "safe" aren't opposites if you invest in the right guard rails.
